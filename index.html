MY WORK

Here are some of my latest work.


This is a sample video of my Color Blind-mode that I implemented in a schoolproject.
During the development of this mode I got to learn a lot about different types of visual impairments and what differs anatomically compared to a person with regular vision. I chose to implement four types of colorblindness; protanopia, tritanopia, deuteranopia and monochromacy (total color blindness). The types of colorblindness posed different challenges as each type has difficulty seeing different colors/nuances. The human eye contains three types of "cone" cells, each of which is able to sense different wavelengths of light. In order for me to be able to simulate these types of conditions, I need to manipulate the values of these cones. I begin by converting the sRGB values of a material into XYZ space and then, using the Hunt-Pointer-Estevez model, I convert them into LMS space (long, medium and short wavelengths of light). The first three types of colorblindness are all missing a different cone which I simulate by regulating the LMS-values. When finished, I reconvert them to sRGB-values and apply them to the post-processing filter. In monochromacy I don't need to convert to LMS-values and can multiply the RGB-values with a simulation vector for achromatopsia/rod monochromats.
